BlueKit · Nights and weekends

Owning a problem end to end

Context
A small handheld device for psychological first aid — it helps people regulate acute stress in the field, when talking to someone isn't an option.
Challenge
Getting from idea to something testable with no team and no budget.
My approach
I designed and built the hardware myself, through several revisions, then ran an informal 25-person pilot to check whether the core effect was real before investing further.
Trade-offs
Two deliberate ones. I treat the pilot as a signal, not proof — that's the honest reading of a small informal study, and I won't claim more than the evidence supports. And I turned down a tempting pivot to an adjacent product idea to stay focused. Depth over novelty.
Outcome
Working hardware, a pilot result worth formalizing properly, and a national innovation-grant application {{GRANT_STATUS}}. Still early — deliberately.
What it says about how I work
I don't need permission, a team, or a title to start owning a problem. And I don't overclaim.
