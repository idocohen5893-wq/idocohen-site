Deloitte · Risk Advisory · 2024–present

Risk, regulation and executive decisions

Context

Risk management consulting for financial services, fintech and payments companies — enterprise risk, fraud risk, compliance and governance.

Challenge

Executives in regulated industries don't lack analysis; they lack a clear line from regulatory complexity to a decision they can defend — across many stakeholders, tight timelines and incomplete information.

What I did

Fraud risk assessment for a global payments technology company. Enterprise risk management for an international fintech organization. Compliance and regulatory work for a major financial services provider. Stakeholder interviews, qualitative and quantitative analysis, and reporting built to support executive decisions — ending in governance frameworks and workplans, not just findings.

What it demonstrates

I make complexity decision-ready — and I'm comfortable being the person in the room who asks which decision the analysis is actually serving.

How I work

Three rules I actually follow.

Clarity before motion.

The first deliverable is the question, sharply put. In risk engagements the breakthrough is rarely more analysis — it's agreeing which decision the analysis serves. I start there.

Ownership without waiting for permission.

Nobody assigned me BlueKit. I saw a problem worth solving, built the MVP, and validated it with real users before asking anyone for anything.

Execution that survives Monday morning.

As CEO I built governance and performance mechanisms so the organization ran on rhythms, not heroics. If it only works when I'm in the room, I'm not done.
